# Brand Guidelines
Create brand guidelines for a modern patient portal website with a visual system that feels simple, light, airy, trustworthy, calm, and caring. The product should feel like a premium digital healthcare experience that reduces stress and supports confidence. It should never feel dense, cold, overly clinical, corporate, dark, or visually heavy.

Brand goals:
- build trust immediately
- create a sense of comfort, peace of mind, and care
- feel modern and polished without being flashy
- feel calm and spacious rather than busy or packed
- support readability, clarity, and emotional ease
- help patients feel safe, guided, and in control

Primary accent color:
- Primary accent: #2A9DFF

Color direction:
Build a light, trustworthy color palette around #2A9DFF. The palette should feel clean, soft, fresh, and calming. Use plenty of light neutrals and soft background tones. Accent colors should support hierarchy gently without making the interface feel loud or intense.

Suggested palette approach:
- Primary accent blue: #2A9DFF
- Soft sky tint: #EAF6FF
- Very light blue background wash: #F5FAFF
- Crisp white: #FFFFFF
- Soft cloud gray: #F6F8FB
- Light border gray: #DCE5EF
- Medium neutral text gray: #5F6B7A
- Dark text charcoal: #1F2A37
- Success accent, soft and reassuring: #67C59A
- Warning accent, soft not alarming: #F4C46A
- Error accent, muted and calm: #E8887D

Color usage rules:
- Use white and very pale blue-gray backgrounds as the foundation
- Use #2A9DFF as the main interactive accent for key actions, links, focus states, and selected navigation
- Use soft tinted surfaces for cards, highlights, and gentle section emphasis
- Keep high contrast for text and critical actions, but avoid harsh visual intensity
- Use status colors in a softened, premium way rather than bright, saturated alert colors
- Avoid dark heavy backgrounds, neon accents, oversaturated colors, and too many competing hues
- Use color sparingly to guide attention, not to decorate every surface

Emotional design intent:
The visual language should communicate:
- trust
- calm
- clarity
- empathy
- stability
- professionalism
- warmth without sentimentality

The experience should feel like:
- a trusted healthcare provider
- a calm waiting room with natural light
- a thoughtful digital assistant
- a clean and elevated consumer product

It should not feel like:
- a hospital back-end system
- an insurance portal
- a crowded dashboard
- a fintech product
- a dark enterprise application
- a flashy wellness brand

Layout and spacing principles:
The entire interface should feel open, breathable, and easy to process.
- Use generous white space throughout
- Create strong spacing between sections, cards, and content groups
- Favor fewer elements per screen
- Keep screens visually balanced and uncluttered
- Do not overload the user with too many competing cards or dense text blocks
- Use clear grouping and hierarchy so each section feels easy to understand at a glance
- Prioritize one primary action and a small number of secondary actions per view
- Maintain a calm rhythm in the layout with consistent margins, padding, and spacing
- Let the design breathe

Surface and card style:
- Use soft card-based layouts
- Cards should feel lightweight, clean, and approachable
- Use rounded corners with a modern but restrained radius
- Use subtle shadows or elevation, very soft and diffuse
- Keep borders light and minimal
- Avoid sharp, harsh containers or dense boxed-in layouts
- Prefer soft layering over strong separation lines

Typography direction:
Typography should feel clean, legible, and quietly confident.
- Use a modern sans serif typeface with excellent readability
- Prioritize legibility over personality
- Use clear hierarchy through size, weight, and spacing
- Keep headings concise and calm
- Body text should be easy to scan, never cramped
- Favor sentence case over all caps
- Avoid overly compressed or overly stylized fonts
- The typography should feel human and approachable, not technical or corporate

Typography behavior:
- Use larger heading sizes to create openness and clarity
- Use comfortable line height
- Keep paragraph widths readable
- Limit the number of type styles
- Use emphasis sparingly
- Avoid visual noise from too many weights, colors, or text treatments

Imagery and illustration direction:
If imagery or illustration is used, it should reinforce trust and calm.
- Use clean, minimal, human-centered visuals
- Show care, support, and reassurance
- Favor soft lighting, natural tones, and authentic healthcare moments
- Avoid overly staged stock-photo energy
- Avoid dramatic medical imagery, intimidating equipment, or high-stress visuals
- If using illustration, keep it minimal, gentle, and modern
- Illustrations should support comprehension and emotional ease, not distract

Iconography:
- Use simple, rounded, modern icons
- Icons should feel clear, calm, and easy to recognize
- Keep stroke weight consistent
- Avoid overly technical or decorative icon styles
- Pair icons with labels for clarity
- Use icons to support navigation and scannability, not as visual clutter

Component behavior:
Buttons:
- Primary buttons should use #2A9DFF
- Primary buttons should feel prominent but not loud
- Secondary buttons should be light, clean, and understated
- Keep button labels short, direct, and reassuring
- Buttons should have generous padding and medium-large touch targets

Inputs and search:
- Search and form fields should feel open and lightweight
- Use soft borders and subtle fills
- Focus states should use the primary accent clearly
- Avoid heavy outlines or crowded field layouts

Alerts and notifications:
- Use soft-tinted backgrounds with clear but calm emphasis
- Important alerts should be noticeable without feeling alarming
- Status messaging should be easy to scan and emotionally steady

Navigation:
- Navigation should feel simple, clear, and spacious
- Use icon and label combinations where helpful
- Keep touch targets medium to large
- Highlight the active state with restrained use of the primary accent
- Avoid crowded menus and excessive depth

Content density rules:
- Keep each screen focused on the most important information only
- Reduce visual and cognitive load
- Show what matters now, then allow deeper access if needed
- Avoid long walls of text
- Avoid presenting too many cards, modules, or data points at once
- Design each screen to support quick understanding and calm decision-making

Accessibility and readability:
- Maintain strong text contrast
- Ensure all interactive elements are easy to identify
- Support large tap targets on both desktop and mobile
- Use clear labels and hierarchy
- Avoid relying only on color to communicate meaning
- Design for users who may be stressed, tired, distracted, or visually strained

Responsive design guidance:
- Preserve the light, airy feeling across desktop and mobile
- On mobile, simplify further rather than compressing everything tightly
- Maintain spacing and hierarchy even on smaller screens
- Avoid dense stacking of too many modules
- Keep the most important actions visible and easy to reach

Overall design instruction:
Design the patient portal as a calm, spacious, trustworthy healthcare experience centered on clarity and emotional ease. Use #2A9DFF as the primary accent within a soft, light, minimal color system. Favor openness, white space, gentle contrast, soft surfaces, and focused content. Every screen should feel easy to scan, emotionally reassuring, and free from clutter. The final experience should communicate trust, comfort, peace of mind, care, and modern simplicity.

## Logo Rules
Name of company is VALDERRAMA HEALTH. Logo is simple, modern, and elegant.

Create logo usage guidelines for a modern patient portal brand. The logo should always feel clean, calm, trustworthy, and professional. Treat it as a premium healthcare mark that must remain simple, legible, and consistent across desktop, mobile, marketing, and product interfaces.

Brand intent:
The logo should communicate trust, care, clarity, and reassurance. It should feel modern and approachable, never playful, cluttered, overly corporate, or overly clinical.

Primary logo color:
- Primary brand blue: #2A9DFF

Approved logo color variations:
- Full color logo using #2A9DFF on white or very light backgrounds
- White logo on solid #2A9DFF backgrounds
- Dark charcoal logo on very light neutral backgrounds when one-color reproduction is needed
- Soft gray or muted dark neutral version only for low-emphasis applications when brand blue is not appropriate

Approved background usage:
- White
- Very pale blue
- Very light neutral gray
- Solid primary blue for reversed white logo use

Background rules:
- Maintain strong contrast at all times
- Use the logo only on clean, uncluttered backgrounds
- Avoid placing the logo on busy photography, complex illustrations, gradients, textured surfaces, or low-contrast backgrounds
- If the logo is placed over imagery, use a clean solid container or reserved clear area behind it

Sizing conventions:
- The logo must always remain legible and visually balanced
- Use the logo at a size large enough for all shapes, symbols, and letterforms to remain clear
- Never scale the logo so small that details become hard to recognize

Recommended minimum sizes:
- Digital full logo with wordmark: no smaller than 120 pixels wide
- Digital symbol or icon only: no smaller than 24 pixels wide or tall
- Mobile header use: typically 24 to 32 pixels tall
- Desktop header use: typically 28 to 40 pixels tall
- Favicon or app icon use: use approved symbol-only version optimized for small sizes

Clear space:
- Always preserve clear space around the logo
- Minimum clear space should equal at least the height of the logo symbol or a key unit within the mark
- No text, icons, borders, or other interface elements should enter this protected area
- Give the logo extra breathing room whenever possible to preserve the light and airy brand feel

Placement guidance:
- Align the logo cleanly within headers, navigation bars, onboarding screens, sign-in pages, and marketing areas
- Prefer top left placement for website headers unless there is a strong brand reason otherwise
- Keep placement consistent across screen sizes
- Use the full logo in primary brand moments and symbol-only mark in constrained spaces where the wordmark would be too small

Logo versions to prepare:
- Full horizontal logo
- Stacked logo if needed for square or narrow spaces
- Symbol-only mark for app icons, favicons, and compact interface use
- Reversed white version for dark or brand-blue backgrounds
- Single-color dark neutral version for limited-use applications

Visual behavior:
- The logo should appear crisp, flat, and simple
- Use flat color only
- Do not add gradients, glows, bevels, shadows, textures, outlines, or three-dimensional effects
- Do not decorate the logo or place it inside unnecessary shapes unless using an approved app icon lockup

What not to do:
- Do not stretch the logo horizontally or vertically
- Do not rotate, skew, distort, crop, or tilt the logo
- Do not change the approved colors
- Do not apply gradients, drop shadows, patterns, or effects
- Do not place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
- Do not add extra icons, symbols, or text to the logo
- Do not rearrange the logo elements
- Do not place the logo inside tight containers with insufficient clear space
- Do not use the symbol and wordmark independently in a way that breaks brand consistency unless using approved lockups
- Do not outline the logo
- Do not use low-opacity versions that weaken legibility
- Do not place the logo too small to read
- Do not use unapproved color combinations
- Do not use the logo as a repeated decorative pattern
- Do not animate the logo in a flashy or distracting way

Tone of use:
The logo should always feel calm, elevated, and reassuring. It should support an interface that is light, spacious, modern, and emotionally steady.

Accessibility and clarity:
- Ensure the logo has enough contrast against its background
- Preserve legibility at all supported screen sizes
- Test small-size versions on mobile and high-density screens
- Use simplified symbol-only assets when needed for very small placements

Overall instruction:
Use the logo with restraint and consistency. Keep it simple, flat, spacious, and highly legible. Every application should reinforce trust, care, calm, and clarity. The logo should never feel crowded, over-styled, distorted, or visually heavy.

## Iconography
Create a consistent iconography system for a modern patient portal website. The icon style should feel calm, clear, trustworthy, lightweight, and professional. Icons must support a healthcare experience that is simple, modern, reassuring, and easy to understand. They should never feel overly technical, harsh, decorative, playful, or visually heavy.

Overall icon style:
- Use a clean, minimalist, flat icon style
- Prefer simple geometric forms with soft, human-friendly curves
- Keep icons visually light and airy
- Use a modern outline-based style or a very minimal flat filled style, but choose one system and use it consistently across the product
- Prioritize clarity and immediate recognition over novelty
- Icons should feel premium and thoughtfully designed, not generic, cartoonish, or overly corporate

Recommended direction:
- Use rounded outline icons with a consistent stroke weight
- Keep corners slightly rounded rather than sharp
- Use simple shapes and limited internal detail
- Avoid excessive lines, tiny details, and visual complexity
- Design icons so they remain readable at small sizes
- Use icons as supportive cues, not as decorative illustrations

Stroke and construction rules:
- Use a consistent stroke weight across all icons
- Keep line endings and corner treatments consistent
- Maintain a consistent visual grid and proportion system
- Balance all icons so they feel like part of the same family
- Use similar optical weight across different symbols
- Keep spacing inside icons open and uncluttered
- Avoid mixing thin delicate icons with heavy bold icons

Shape language:
- Use rounded rectangles, soft circles, and gently curved lines
- Favor approachable, soft geometry that feels caring and stable
- Avoid aggressive angles, sharp spikes, or rigid technical shapes
- Make sure icons feel friendly but still professional and credible

Detail level:
- Keep icons simple and easy to scan
- Show only the essential visual information needed to communicate the concept
- Remove unnecessary details, textures, depth, or decoration
- Avoid complex metaphors that require interpretation
- Use familiar healthcare and interface symbols that users can understand instantly

Color usage:
- Use flat color only
- Primary icon color should usually be a dark neutral or the primary accent color when emphasis is needed
- Approved primary accent color: #2A9DFF
- Icons may also appear in charcoal, muted gray, white on solid blue, or very light tinted backgrounds
- Do not use gradients, shadows, glow effects, textures, or multicolor icon treatments unless explicitly creating a special illustration system
- Use color sparingly to signal emphasis, status, or selected states

Filled versus outline use:
- Default to outline icons for most interface elements because they feel lighter and more open
- Use filled icons only for selected states, active navigation, or very small icons where outline clarity may break down
- If using both outline and filled states, ensure they are clearly related versions of the same icon family

Sizing and spacing:
- Design icons to work clearly at small and medium interface sizes
- Common interface icon sizes should feel balanced at 16, 20, 24, and 32 pixels
- Maintain consistent padding around icons in buttons, cards, navigation, and alerts
- Do not crowd icons too close to labels or edges
- Ensure icons remain crisp and recognizable on both desktop and mobile

Accessibility and usability:
- Pair icons with text labels for navigation and important actions
- Never rely on icons alone for critical meaning
- Ensure high contrast against backgrounds
- Make icons easy to distinguish for users under stress or with limited visual attention
- Keep symbols familiar and intuitive
- Optimize for fast scanning and low cognitive load

Healthcare-specific guidance:
- Use symbols that feel caring, respectful, and reassuring
- For medical concepts, choose the simplest possible recognizable metaphor
- Avoid overly clinical, intimidating, or technical medical imagery
- Avoid icons that feel emergency-focused unless the context truly requires urgency
- Keep healthcare symbols human-centered and supportive

Examples of the desired icon feeling:
- calm
- rounded
- modern
- precise
- open
- clear
- supportive
- premium
- understated

Avoid these icon traits:
- sharp or aggressive geometry
- inconsistent stroke widths
- crowded internal details
- 3D effects
- skeuomorphic styling
- glossy or gradient treatments
- cartoon-like character
- decorative flourishes
- mixed icon systems
- highly abstract symbols that are hard to recognize
- generic stock icon feel

Usage across the website:
- Use icons consistently in navigation, dashboard cards, quick actions, status areas, alerts, empty states, and assistant entry points
- Keep icon treatment uniform across all pages and devices
- The icon system should reinforce the overall brand feeling of trust, comfort, peace of mind, care, and simplicity

Overall instruction:
Design all icons as part of one cohesive family. Keep them flat, minimal, rounded, and highly legible. The system should feel light and modern, with enough warmth and softness to support a healthcare experience centered on trust and calm.

## Spacing & Grid
Create layout and spacing guidelines for a modern patient portal website that feels light, airy, calm, trustworthy, and easy to use. The layout system should create a strong sense of white space, comfort, peace of mind, care, and relaxation. It should never feel cramped, dense, heavy, or overly structured. Every screen should feel open, breathable, and emotionally reassuring while still remaining highly functional and clear.

Overall layout intent:
- prioritize spaciousness and clarity
- use white space generously
- reduce visual and cognitive load
- create calm rhythm across the interface
- support trust, comfort, and ease
- avoid tight layouts, crowded cards, or compressed content
- let content breathe

Base unit:
- Use an 8 pixel base unit system for layout, spacing, and component rhythm
- Allow 4 pixel increments only for fine adjustments inside smaller interface elements
- All padding, margins, gaps, and component dimensions should align to this system to keep the layout feeling ordered and consistent

Spacing scale:
Use a soft, generous spacing scale that reinforces openness and readability.
Recommended spacing tokens:
- 4 pixels for very tight internal adjustments only
- 8 pixels for micro spacing between small related elements
- 12 pixels for compact internal spacing when needed
- 16 pixels for standard spacing inside cards and between closely related content
- 24 pixels for comfortable spacing between sections within a component
- 32 pixels for major spacing between cards or content groups
- 40 pixels for spacious separation on smaller screens
- 48 pixels for major section spacing on desktop
- 64 pixels for large page-level breathing room
- 80 pixels or more for especially open hero or top-level dashboard spacing when appropriate

Spacing behavior:
- default to more space rather than less
- maintain consistent vertical rhythm
- separate unrelated content clearly
- avoid stacking too many elements with minimal spacing
- use spacing to communicate hierarchy before relying on borders or color blocks
- create visual pause between modules and actions
- preserve a feeling of calm and order

Column grid:
Use a responsive grid that supports clean alignment while preserving openness.

Desktop grid:
- Use a 12-column grid
- Keep column widths flexible
- Design common content areas to span 4, 6, 8, or 12 columns
- Avoid filling every available column with content
- Leave intentional negative space where possible

Tablet grid:
- Use an 8-column grid

Mobile grid:
- Use a 4-column grid

Grid philosophy:
- the grid should organize content without making the page feel rigid
- do not force content to occupy every grid opportunity
- preserve air and simplicity even when using a structured system
- favor fewer, stronger content modules rather than many competing blocks

Gutters:
Use generous gutters so columns do not feel crowded.

Recommended gutters:
- Desktop: 24 to 32 pixels
- Tablet: 24 pixels
- Mobile: 16 to 20 pixels

Gutter behavior:
- maintain enough separation for cards and content modules to feel distinct
- do not reduce gutters so much that the interface feels compressed
- on larger screens, prefer wider gutters to reinforce the airy brand feel

Margins:
Use generous outer margins to frame content and keep the layout from feeling edge-to-edge or crowded.

Recommended page margins:
- Desktop: 48 to 80 pixels depending on viewport width
- Tablet: 32 to 40 pixels
- Mobile: 16 to 24 pixels

Margin behavior:
- content should not press too close to the edge of the screen
- preserve a comfortable content frame around all main layouts
- allow more generous margins on larger screens to create calm and elegance
- keep mobile margins compact but still breathable

Container width:
- Use a max content width so lines of text and modules do not become too wide
- Keep primary content containers comfortable for reading and scanning
- Favor centered content zones with spacious side margins on larger screens
- Avoid ultra-wide layouts that make the portal feel sparse in a disconnected way
- Balance openness with cohesion

Recommended max widths:
- Primary content container: around 1200 to 1280 pixels
- Text-heavy content areas: narrower for readability
- Dashboard modules may use wider layouts but should still maintain clear spacing and grouping

Card spacing and module layout:
- Use card-based layouts with generous internal padding
- Standard card padding should usually be 16 to 24 pixels on mobile and 24 to 32 pixels on desktop
- Maintain clear separation between cards with at least 16 to 24 pixels, increasing to 24 to 32 pixels for major dashboard modules
- Do not pack too many cards into one row
- Prefer two to three strong modules per row on desktop rather than dense multi-card matrices
- Let each card feel calm, self-contained, and easy to scan

Vertical rhythm:
- Use consistent spacing between headings, body text, metadata, controls, and supporting information
- Give headings room above and below
- Do not crowd buttons directly against text blocks
- Create gentle, predictable rhythm so users can scan comfortably
- Use larger vertical spacing between sections than within sections

Density rules:
- keep each screen focused
- show only the most important information first
- avoid dashboards overloaded with many equally weighted panels
- prioritize fewer modules with stronger hierarchy
- use progressive disclosure for secondary details
- maintain visual simplicity even when there is a lot of information available

Responsive spacing principles:
- preserve openness across all breakpoints
- do not simply compress desktop spacing for mobile
- adapt spacing so mobile still feels calm and uncluttered
- stack content with generous separation
- keep touch targets and content blocks comfortably spaced
- maintain hierarchy through spacing, not just scale

Use of white space:
- white space is a core part of the brand experience, not empty leftover space
- use it intentionally to create focus, calm, and confidence
- let important actions stand out because surrounding content is restrained
- allow breathing room around navigation, search, cards, alerts, and page sections
- avoid filling every open area with extra content or decoration

What not to do:
- do not use tight grids with narrow gutters
- do not place too many cards in one row
- do not reduce margins so much that content feels edge-to-edge
- do not use cramped internal padding
- do not overload dashboards with too many modules at once
- do not create long dense stacks with little separation
- do not rely on dividers and borders where spacing can do the work better
- do not make mobile layouts feel compressed or crowded

Overall instruction:
Build the layout system with generous spacing, a clear responsive grid, and a strong sense of white space. Use an 8 pixel base unit, spacious gutters, comfortable margins, and open card layouts to create a healthcare experience that feels calm, premium, supportive, and easy to process. Every screen should communicate comfort, peace, care, and relaxation through spacing and layout alone.
